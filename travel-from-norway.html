1. Getting to London from Norway

Norway offers a range of travel options for getting to London, making it relatively easy and affordable. Here are the main routes:

Direct Flights

  • Cities with Direct Flights: Oslo, Bergen, Stavanger, and Trondheim have direct flights to London’s major airports (Heathrow, Gatwick, and Stansted).
  • Airlines: Norwegian, SAS, and British Airways are popular choices, with Ryanair offering budget-friendly options.
  • Average Ticket Prices:
    • Low season (January - March, October - November): 400-800 NOK (one-way) with budget airlines.
    • Peak season (Summer, December holidays): 1,200-2,000 NOK, higher if booking late.
  • Booking Tips: Booking 1-2 months in advance can save up to 50%. Use Skyscanner, Google Flights, or Momondo for the best deals.

Budget Option: Indirect Flights or Bus/Ferry Combos

Consider combining flights to nearby cities like Amsterdam or Brussels with a train or bus to London. For a scenic option, DFDS Seaways operates ferries from Norway to the UK.

2. Navigating London: Public Transportation Tips

Oyster Card and Contactless Payments

  • Oyster Card: Buy for £7 deposit and load with funds for discounted fares across Tube, buses, and trains.
  • Contactless Payment: Many travelers use debit/credit cards with contactless feature. Daily fare caps offer budget-friendly options.

Cost Overview

  • Single Tube Ticket: £2.40 (Oyster/contactless, zones 1-2)
  • Daily Cap: £7.70 for unlimited travel within zones 1-2
  • Bus Fare: £1.75 per ride, daily cap of £5.25
  • Travelcard: A 7-day Travelcard for central London costs around £40, ideal for longer stays.

3. Accommodation in London: Finding Budget-Friendly Options

Budget Stays

  • Hostels: Rates from £20/night. Popular options include Generator Hostel (King’s Cross) and YHA London Central.
  • Affordable Hotels: Try Premier Inn or Travelodge, often priced £50-£80/night.

Mid-Range Options

  • Zones 3-4 Hotels: Staying outside central London (Zones 3-4) can save on accommodation while providing easy Tube access.
  • Airbnb: Airbnb rentals in areas like Shoreditch and Camden provide better space and value for groups.

4. Heading to Tottenham’s Stadium (Tottenham Hotspur Stadium)

Travel to the Stadium

  • Tube and Rail: Take the Victoria Line to Seven Sisters Station, then a 20-minute walk or 5-minute bus to the stadium.
  • Bus: Routes 149 and 259 stop near the stadium.

Tickets and Stadium Tour

  • Match Tickets: Prices range from £30 to £100+, based on seating and match. Buy tickets early.
  • Stadium Tour: For around £30, explore locker rooms, the field, and more.

5. Food and Daily Budget Tips

Budget-Friendly Dining

  • Street Markets: Borough Market, Camden Market, and Boxpark Shoreditch offer diverse meals for £5-£10.
  • Supermarkets: Tesco, Sainsbury’s, and Marks & Spencer have meal deals for under £5.
